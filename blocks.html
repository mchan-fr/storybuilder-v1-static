Block Descriptions

A guide to the different block types available in StoryBuilder

Cinematic scroll

When you want text to roll up and over your video. Ideal for video shot in a fixed position that would benefit from text — best kept minimal — to help explain or emphasize what the clip is showing. The text is activated and controlled by user scroll. This block mimics the New York Times' piece on my brother-in-law's Chinese lion heads.

Full-bleed media

When you have a stunning horizontal photo or video that you want desktop users to see in all its glory. The full-bleed displays your visual in the browser's full width. Also a good block to add visual variety if the preceding block was text or displayed vertical images or videos.

Gallery

When you have 2 to 4 photos or clips that collectively capture a moment, time, theme, etc., and individually don't warrant their own block. Pick from preset configurations based on the number of visuals. If you have more than 4, stack a gallery block on top of another.

Hero-horizontal

When you want to start with a bang. If you have an horizontal image or super-short video clip that captures the vibe of your piece, this is your go-to for the opening block. Pair your visual with an engaging headline and deck to draw in your audience and get them scrolling.

Hero-vertical

When you want to start with a bang and have the optimal vertical image or super-short video. Similar to the Hero-horizontal, this can be an effective opening block with the right vertical visual and vertical headline.

Media-horizontal

When you have a horizontal photo or video, but it doesn't warrant the full-bleed treatment. Choose from different widths and follow it with story text.
